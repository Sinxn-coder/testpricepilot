Terms

Terms for using the PricePilot API and dashboard.

These terms describe how the service may be used, where responsibility stays with the customer, and the operational limits that apply to platform access.

Last updated: March 23, 2026

1. API usage and licensing

PricePilot grants you a revocable, non-exclusive right to use the API and dashboard for legitimate pricing, tax, and analytics workflows tied to your own business operations.

  • You are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of your API keys.
  • The service is intended for real product and business use, not abusive or destructive automation.
  • Usage limits may apply depending on the plan attached to your account.

2. Optimization responsibility

PricePilot provides tax-aware pricing and rounding recommendations, but the final commercial decision remains with the merchant using the tool.

You should verify that optimized prices fit your specific legal, tax, and business requirements before presenting them to customers.

3. Service limitations

The service is provided as operational software. While reliability is a goal, uninterrupted availability and universal legal accuracy cannot be guaranteed in every environment.

  • Platform downtime, maintenance periods, and third-party dependency issues may affect availability.
  • Incorrect request inputs can lead to pricing results that do not reflect your intended configuration.

4. Suspension and termination

PricePilot may suspend or terminate access for abuse, fraud, or repeated violations of platform rules. Customers may stop using the service and rotate or remove API access as needed.
